A build-along book · 12 chapters

Turn a phone into a controller your PC can't tell from plastic

Start knowing nothing about drivers, sockets, or input APIs. Finish having built a working gamepad server — and understanding every layer of why it works.

How to use this book

Chapters are meant to be read in order and one at a time — each assumes only what came before it. Nothing is skipped or dumbed down; the harder material is simply placed later, once you have the vocabulary for it.

Every chapter ends with a recap listing what you now know. If a recap point feels shaky, reread that chapter before moving on — the next one will lean on it.

The ▸ rows are optional depth: extra code, edge cases, history. Skip them on a first pass and come back when you're building.

About this book. It reconstructs the architecture of DroidJoy, a closed-source Windows + Android product, using its public documentation plus the open-source projects that solve the identical problem — ViGEmBus, PadConnect, Joy2DroidX, and VirtualGamePad. None of DroidJoy's internals are confirmed by its author.

Latency figures throughout are approximate ranges for typical consumer hardware, shown for proportion rather than as measurements.
